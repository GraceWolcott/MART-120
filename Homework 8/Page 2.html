For you, dear traveler, two poems from my repertoire:


Wisdom Teeth

I’m not getting my wisdom teeth out.

I don’t care if they kill me.

I don’t care if they push my teeth all about,

and make my smile ugly.


I don’t care if they grow in all crooked,

and end up all rotten.

I don’t care if you take one look and

think an infection I’ve gotten.


I am not getting my wisdom teeth out,

no matter what the dentist said.

This whole thing is a scam, I have no doubt,

those teeth aren’t coming out of my head.

My Poems Suck

There is nothing special about my poems.

The rhyming scheme’s always the same.

My writing style is obnoxious,

Shel Silverstein puts me to shame.


My funny poems are barely funny,

and only in a weird way.

The topics are never very deep,

I throw metaphors away.


Sure, you can cry me a river,

but my poems won’t bring you to tears.

If you want something with originality,

you should go read Shakespeare's.
